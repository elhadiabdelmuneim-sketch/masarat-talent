The Problem

Skilled. Displaced.
Invisible to employers.

Across Sudan, South Sudan, Ethiopia, DRC and beyond — millions of experienced engineers, nurses, IT specialists and finance professionals are working below their potential. Not because of lack of skill. Because the infrastructure that connects talent to opportunity never reached them.

"The problem isn't talent.
The problem is the bridge."
🏛️
No formal address or recognised identity International employers can't verify or onboard professionals without stable documentation.
💳
Payment barriers 40 of 61 African countries exclude displaced people from formal banking and international transfers.
🌐
No trusted pathway to vetted remote work Generic platforms like LinkedIn or Upwork were built for people who already have infrastructure.
📋
Skills not packaged for international standards Years of real-world experience can't be communicated in formats GCC or European employers expect.
The Solution

Three steps to opportunity.

Masarat Talent is designed as a structured pathway — not just a job board. From first profile to first placement.

01
Prepare Profile

Build a professional profile that meets international standards. CV, credentials, skills assessment — packaged for GCC and European employer expectations.

02
Match Opportunities

Curated, vetted remote job opportunities matched to your profile — from employers who are specifically open to hiring displaced professionals.

03
Place End-to-End

Full support through the hiring process — interview preparation, contract review, payment setup, and onboarding assistance. Until you're working.

About the Founder

Built by someone who understands displacement.

Abdelmoneim Elhadi Nooh is a Sudanese engineer and operations professional with 9+ years of experience across manufacturing, supply chain, and digital transformation. He holds an MSc in International Management (Business Analytics & Digital Transformation) and a Six Sigma Black Belt.

Masarat Talent was born from a simple observation: the people he knew — skilled, experienced, hardworking — were invisible to the international job market. Not because they lacked ability. Because the system was never designed for their reality.

The platform is being developed under the Tony Elumelu Foundation (TEF) 2026 Entrepreneurship Programme, where Abdelmoneim was selected from over 3,200 applicants across Africa. It is registered in Rwanda as Masarat Talent Limited.
